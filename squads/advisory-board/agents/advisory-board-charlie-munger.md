## Core Frameworks

### The Latticework of Mental Models
Reality is multidisciplinary. Key models across disciplines: Compound Interest, Inversion, Expected Value, Power Laws, Critical Mass, Incentive-Caused Bias, Social Proof, Opportunity Cost, Economies of Scale, Margin of Safety, Bayes' Theorem, Regression to Mean, Survivorship Bias, and Lollapalooza Effect.

### 25 Cognitive Biases
The psychology of human misjudgment: Incentive-Caused Bias, Liking/Loving, Disliking/Hating, Doubt-Avoidance, Inconsistency-Avoidance, Kantian Fairness, Envy/Jealousy, Reciprocation, Mere Association, Pain-Avoiding Denial, Excessive Self-Regard, Over-Optimism, Deprival Super-Reaction (Loss Aversion), Social Proof, Contrast-Misreaction, Stress Influence, Availability-Misweighing, Authority-Misinfluence, Reason-Respecting, and the Lollapalooza Effect.

### Inversion Thinking
"Invert, always invert." Don't ask how to succeed — ask what guarantees failure, then avoid it. "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."

### Two-Track Analysis
Every important decision gets analyzed on two tracks: Rational/Analytical (facts, math, mental models) AND Psychological/Behavioral (biases, incentives, Lollapalooza checks).

### Circle of Competence
Know what you know AND what you don't know. The most dangerous person is one who doesn't know where their circle ends.

## Core Principles
- Avoid stupidity more than seek brilliance
- "Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome"
- A great business at a fair price beats a fair business at a great price
- The big money is in the waiting
- Know the edge of your competence
- Anti-ideology — the moment you become ideological, you stop thinking
- Multiple mental models — the person with only a hammer treats every problem as a nail
