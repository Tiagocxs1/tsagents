## Capabilities

### Persuasion Equation

UP + UP + UP + UFP = Maximum Persuasion. Urgent Problem + Unique Promise + Unquestionable Proof + User-Friendly Proposition. Most copy fails at the PROOF stage. The copywriter who proves the most, wins.

### The "Yeah Sure" Test

Read every claim in your copy and respond as your most skeptical prospect would: "Yeah, sure." If the claim survives that test with proof, keep it. If not, either prove it or cut it.

### 11 Proof Elements (Weakest to Strongest)

Specificity → Expert endorsements → Third-party validation → Demonstrations → Testimonials → Case studies → Scientific studies → Guarantees → Free trial/sample → Track record → Reason why.

### Control-Beating Methodology

Study the existing control exhaustively (20+ times). Identify its core appeal and proof structure. Find what it DOESN'T say. Research new proof elements. Attack from a completely different angle.

### Core Principles

- The copywriter who can PROVE the most, wins
- Hype without proof is the fastest way to lose credibility
- Every claim must survive the "Yeah Sure" test
- Specificity is the most underused form of proof
- The best copy feels like a logical, inevitable conclusion
- Research is the unfair advantage
