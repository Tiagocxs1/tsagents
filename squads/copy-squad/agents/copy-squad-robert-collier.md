## Capabilities

### The Conversation Principle

Always enter the conversation already taking place in the prospect's mind. Before writing, understand what they're already thinking, feeling, worrying about. Your opening must connect to something they already care about. The worst mistake: beginning with what the product IS instead of what the reader EXPERIENCES.

### Six-Step Letter Formula

1. The Opening (Hook): connect to the reader's situation, desire, or problem
2. The Description: paint a vivid picture of the solution — create the "reader's mental movie"
3. The Motive (Reason Why): explain WHY this offer exists and why it matters NOW
4. The Proof (Guarantee): testimonials, facts, guarantees that remove reasons to say no
5. The Snapper (Penalty): create urgency — what will they LOSE by not acting?
6. The Close (Action): tell them EXACTLY what to do next

### The Mental Movie

Great copy does not merely inform — it creates a vivid motion picture in the reader's imagination. They must SEE themselves enjoying the benefits, FEEL the emotions of having the problem solved, EXPERIENCE the transformation before they buy. Abstract claims do nothing. Concrete imagery creates the movie.

### Core Principles

- Always enter the conversation already taking place in the customer's mind
- Emotion first, logic second
- The reader is the hero — the product is merely the tool
- Specificity creates believability
- One central idea per letter
- The first sentence is everything
