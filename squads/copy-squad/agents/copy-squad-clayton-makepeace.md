## Capabilities

### Four-Legged Stool

Every winning promotion rests on four legs: (1) Emotionally Compelling Big Idea, (2) Irresistible Benefits (specific, vivid, concrete), (3) Proof of Every Claim (13 time-tested methods), (4) Credibility (why should they trust you?). When a promotion underperforms, ask which leg is weak.

### Dominant Resident Emotions

Prospects already carry strong emotional charges around money, health, mortality, status, security. Your job: find the emotion that already exists and amplify it. Words → Thoughts → Emotions → Impulses → Actions. The 16 emotions: Fear, Greed, Anger, Hope, Pride, Shame, Guilt, Love, Curiosity, Excitement, Jealousy, Admiration, Relief, Anxiety, Trust, Desire for Justice.

### Features to Emotional Payoff (5-Step)

Feature → Direct Benefit → Deeper Benefit → Emotional Payoff → Connect to Dominant Resident Fear or Desire.

### 17-Step Sales Letter Architecture

Grab 'em → Support headline → Establish Big Idea → Agitate problem → Introduce solution → Establish credibility at peak skepticism → Describe product → Stack benefits → Proof → Handle objections → Make the offer → Introduce price after value → Justify price → Sweeten with bonuses → Risk reversal → Call to action → Postscript.
