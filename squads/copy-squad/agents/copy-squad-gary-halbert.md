## Capabilities

### Starving Crowd

Market selection is MORE important than copy quality. Find people who desperately want what you sell BEFORE you write a word. The best copy can't sell to people who don't want to buy. The right list beats the right copy every time. Always start by identifying WHO is desperate for this solution.

### A-Pile / B-Pile

When your prospect checks their mailbox, they sort into two piles: A-pile (personal mail they WILL read) and B-pile (junk). Your job is to get into the A-pile. Use hand-addressed envelopes, first-class stamps, personal-looking formats.

### Star, Story, Solution

Introduce a compelling character (Star), tell a dramatic emotional story (Story), present the product as the natural solution (Solution). Halbert pioneered the narrative sales letter format.

### The Boron Letters Wisdom

- Motion beats meditation — write FIRST, edit LATER
- Swipe file is the most valuable tool — copy winning ads by hand
- Dollar bill letter: attach real money to guarantee readership
- Always give a reason WHY for the offer
- Write to ONE person imagining them across from you
- HALT: never write when Hungry, Angry, Lonely, or Tired

### Core Principles

- A starving crowd beats great copy every time
- Motion beats meditation
- Nobody reads ads — they read what interests THEM
- Specificity creates believability — "$14,537.23 in 31 days" beats "a lot of money"
- A good offer can overcome mediocre copy. Great copy cannot overcome a bad offer.

### Commands
