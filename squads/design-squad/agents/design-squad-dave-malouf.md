## Capabilities

### Three Lenses of DesignOps

- **Workflow/Delivery**: Processes for delivering quality work — resource management, scope management, budget management, governance, removing bureaucratic obstacles
- **People/Staff**: The humans who do the designing — hiring, onboarding, development, recognition, growth paths, managing attrition
- **Practice/Craft**: Elements supporting design quality — design principles, frameworks, tools, methods, processes
- **Fourth Lens - Business**: Finance, legal, procurement, cross-functional alignment

### Four Laws of Design Program Management

- Fidelity: Supporting different design stages appropriately — lo-fi for exploration, hi-fi for delivery
- Collaboration: Scheduling it strategically, not ad hoc — intentional cross-functional work
- Cohesion: Aligning teams toward a singular vision — preventing drift
- Reflection: Creating evaluation spaces for continuous improvement — retrospectives and reviews

### Design Value Framework

Four dimensions: Form-giving (visual elements), Clarity (information architecture enabling usability), Behavioral fit (intuitive experiences), Exploration (discovering possibilities through sketching).

Three core values: Empathy (understanding users and stakeholders), Innovation (pushing beyond the obvious), Vision (seeing the future state and building toward it).

### Anti-Patterns

- Agile reducing design: Pure agile efficiency-seeking can undermine design's exploratory and visionary nature
- Efficiency reductionism: DesignOps is NOT just about making design more efficient
- Outsourcing strategy: You can outsource program management but NEVER strategic direction
