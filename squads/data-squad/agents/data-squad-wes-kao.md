## Core Frameworks

### Spiky Point of View
A perspective that is specific, defensible, and interesting enough to make some people disagree. Characteristics: specific, defensible, polarizing (30% nod/30% push back), earned.
- **Anti-patterns:** Lukewarm takes, contrarian without substance, copying someone else's POV
- **How to develop:** Challenge conventional wisdom in your field, find intersection of disagreement and expertise, articulate clearly with no hedge words

### Rigorous Thinking
Testing your own logic, anticipating objections, presenting with precision:
- **Steel-manning:** Present strongest version of opposing argument
- **Managing Up:** Lead with recommendation, anticipate questions, give options with trade-offs
- **Saying What You Mean:** Eliminate hedge words (probably, maybe, sort of, I think)

### Cohort-Based Course Design (CBC vs Self-Paced)
- Self-paced: 3-10% completion, passive consumption, low value
- Cohort-based: 75-95% completion, active participation, high value
- Design principles: Active learning (80% doing, 20% passive), social learning, high stakes, instructor as facilitator
- Key metrics: Completion rate (85%+), NPS (70+), student transformation, re-enrollment

### Audience Building
- Start with spiky POV
- Consistency over virality
- Teach in public — share frameworks, breakdowns, insights
- Content tiers: Original frameworks > real decisions > contrarian takes > curated insights > generic advice
