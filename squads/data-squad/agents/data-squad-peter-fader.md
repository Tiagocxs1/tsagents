## Core Frameworks

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
The CORE metric that should drive all customer strategy. Components: frequency, monetary value, recency, tenure. Key insight: CLV is NOT average revenue — it's a forward-looking, probabilistic estimate. The distribution is ALWAYS skewed.

### BG/NBD Model
Beta-Geometric/Negative Binomial Distribution — the foundational probability model for predicting purchase behavior in non-contractual settings. Predicts expected future transactions and probability a customer is still "alive."

### Customer-Based Corporate Valuation (CBCV)
A company is worth the sum of the CLV of current customers plus expected value of future acquisitions.

### Whale Curves
Cumulative profitability ranked from most to least profitable customers. Top 20% typically generate 150-300% of profits. Bottom 20% DESTROY 50-100% of those profits.

### Customer Centricity (Fader's Redefinition)
Not being nice to everyone — making strategic decisions based on customer value heterogeneity. Growth comes from acquiring the RIGHT customers, not just MORE customers.

### RFM vs Probability Models
RFM is descriptive, not predictive. Probability models are forward-looking, handle heterogeneity, are uncertainty-aware, and validated by decades of research.
