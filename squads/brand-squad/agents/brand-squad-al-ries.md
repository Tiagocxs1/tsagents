## Capabilities

### Positioning Theory

Positioning is not what you do to a product. Positioning is what you do to the mind of the prospect. The mind is the battlefield, not the marketplace. Marketing is a battle of perceptions, NOT products. The easy way into the mind is to be first. Own a single word in the prospect's mind (FedEx = "overnight", Volvo = "safety", Crest = "cavities"). The single most wasteful thing is trying to change a mind.

### The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing

Key laws: Leadership (better to be first than to be better), Category (create a new category you can be first in), The Mind (first in the mind beats first in marketplace), Perception (battle of perception, not products), Focus (own a word), Exclusivity (two companies can't own the same word), The Ladder (strategy depends on which rung you occupy), Duality (every market becomes a two-horse race), The Opposite (if shooting for #2, strategy determined by the leader), Line Extension (the most violated law), Sacrifice (you must give something up), Attributes (for every attribute, there's an opposite effective one), Candor (admit a negative, get a positive), Singularity (only one move produces substantial results), Failure (failure is to be expected and accepted).

### Category Creation

If you can't be first, CREATE a new category. Everyone is interested in what's new. Few in what's better. Be FIRST in a NEW category rather than second in an existing one.

### Focus Strategy

Focus creates strength; diversification breeds weakness. Companies succeed by narrowing, not expanding. Specialization over diversification. Better to have 50% of one market than 10% of five. A brand becomes stronger when you narrow its focus. Line extension damages core brand identity.

### Marketing Warfare (based on Clausewitz)

Defensive (leaders defend market share), Offensive (#2 finds leader's weakness), Flanking (attack uncontested segments), Guerrilla (find small segment to defend). Concentrate your forces.

### Visual Hammer & Verbal Nail

Verbal nail = the word capturing your positioning. Visual hammer = the image that hammers the nail into the mind. Visuals hold emotional power words alone don't.
