## Capabilities

### Aaker Brand Equity Model (5 Dimensions)

1. Brand Loyalty: depth of commitment and attachment, reduces marketing costs
2. Brand Awareness: familiarity from recognition to recall to top-of-mind
3. Perceived Quality: consumer assessment of overall quality vs alternatives, enables premium pricing
4. Brand Associations: mental connections — attributes, intangibles, personality, user imagery
5. Proprietary Assets: patents, trademarks, IP, channel relationships

### Brand Vision Model (The Aaker Model)

Brand essence (single central theme), core elements (2-5 most central, timeless), extended elements (3-5 adding texture, may evolve).

Four perspectives: Brand as product (scope, attributes, quality, uses, users), Brand as organization (attributes, local vs global), Brand as person (personality traits, relationships), Brand as symbol (visual imagery, heritage).

### Brand Value Proposition

Functional benefits (tangible utility), Emotional benefits (positive feelings), Self-expressive benefits (how it helps express identity), Social benefits (connection to communities and causes).

### Brand Relationship Spectrum

Branded house (single master brand — Google, Virgin), Sub-brands (master + modifier — Apple iPhone), Endorsed brands (independent + endorser — Marriott's Courtyard), House of brands (standalone, parent invisible — P&G).

### Brand Relevance Framework

Real growth comes from offerings so innovative they create new categories/subcategories, making competitors irrelevant. Brand preference (my brand is better) vs Brand relevance (new category with must-haves).

### Core Principles

- A brand is an asset, not an expense
- A product can be copied; a brand is unique. A product can be outdated; a brand is timeless
- Branding adds spirit and soul to what would otherwise be robotic
- Brand vision should go beyond functional benefits to organizational values, purpose, personality
- Management of brand equity requires patience and vision
- Show, don't tell — treat targets as partners, friends, or mentees
