## Capabilities

### The Five-Phase Brand Identity Process

**Phase 1 - Conducting Research**: Be a sleuth, a shrink, and a scientist. Marketing audit, competitive audit, technology audit, legal audit (trademark), language audit, stakeholder interviews, audit readout.

**Phase 2 - Clarifying Strategy**: Get decision-makers to agree on what the brand is and how it's different. Narrowing focus, brand positioning, brand brief, naming, big idea, attributes and values.

**Phase 3 - Designing Identity**: Visualizing the future. Logo design across mark types, color palette, typography selection, visual identity system, testing across applications.

**Phase 4 - Creating Touchpoints**: Applying identity across all contact points. Business systems, marketing collateral, digital presence, environmental/signage, packaging, employee communications.

**Phase 5 - Managing Assets**: Protecting and governing over time. Brand standards, governance policies, training, ongoing monitoring, asset management, launch planning.

### Nine Brand Identity Ideals

Vision (compelling passionate leadership), Meaning (stand for something), Authenticity (clarity about market and positioning), Differentiation (stand apart), Sustainability (longevity beyond trends), Coherence (every touchpoint reinforces the same message), Flexibility (positions for change), Commitment (active management), Value (measurable results).

### Mark Types

Wordmarks (Google, FedEx), Letterform (McDonald's M), Pictorial (simplified image), Abstract shapes (Nike swoosh), Emblems (Harley-Davidson), Dynamic marks, Characters or mascots.

### Brand Architecture

Monolithic (corporate brand drives all), Endorsed (sub-brands connected to parent), Pluralistic (each brand stands independently).

### Core Principles

- Brand identity fuels recognition, amplifies differentiation, and makes big ideas accessible
- It's not about selling — it's about educating
- It's not your logo — it's their logo. A logo is not a brand
- Design is intelligence made visible
- No one does it alone — collaboration across teams and disciplines
- Research and strategic agreement must come before design begins
- Seize every opportunity to design compelling experiences at every touchpoint
