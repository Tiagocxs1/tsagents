## Capabilities

### The Brand Identity Prism (6 Facets)

1. **Physique**: Tangible characteristics — logo, colors, packaging, product design. The brand's "body."
2. **Personality**: Character traits as if the brand were a person — tone, style, behavior.
3. **Culture**: Values, principles, beliefs underpinning actions — ideological foundation.
4. **Relationship**: Nature of connection between brand and consumers.
5. **Reflection**: How the brand portrays its typical customer (outward mirror).
6. **Self-Image**: How consumers perceive themselves through brand association (internal mirror).

Axes: Vertical (Sender ↔ Receiver), Horizontal (External/Social ↔ Internal/Spirit). All six facets must be coherent and mutually reinforcing.

### Identity vs Image

Identity is what the brand IS (sender). Image is what consumers PERCEIVE (receiver). Brand management must START from identity (sender-side). You cannot manage what you have not defined. Identity precedes image.

### Three-Level Identity System

Apex/Kernel (non-negotiable essence — cannot change without destroying the brand), Middle/Style (communication codes, more flexible), Base/Themes (advertising themes, most flexible). Freedom increases from top to bottom.

### Luxury Strategy

Luxury operates by its own logic — the OPPOSITE of conventional marketing. Six criteria: qualitative hedonistic experience, premium pricing beyond functional value, heritage and savoir-faire, restricted distribution, personalized services, social marker of distinction.

### Dream Equation

Dream = f(Awareness - Penetration). High awareness + low ownership = maximum desirability. Challenge: how to grow while maintaining rarity.

### The 24 Anti-Laws of Luxury Marketing

Key anti-laws: Forget positioning, ensure product has enough flaws to give it soul, don't pander to customers, make it difficult to buy, communicate to those you're NOT targeting, advertising feeds the myth not sales, raise prices over time to increase demand, don't respond to rising demand, don't relocate factories, don't test, don't seek consensus.

### Core Principles

- A brand is not a product — it is the product's essence, meaning, and direction
- Identity precedes image — you cannot manage what you have not defined
- Luxury is superlative, never comparative
- Two ways to go bankrupt: not listening to clients, and listening too much
- Luxury sets the price; price does not set luxury
- Never sacrifice long-term to short-term
