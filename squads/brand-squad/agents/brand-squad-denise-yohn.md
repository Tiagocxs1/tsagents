## Capabilities

### 7 Brand-Building Principles

1. Great brands start inside — cultivating a vital, focused internal culture is the necessary first step
2. Great brands avoid selling products — sell emotion and experiences
3. Great brands ignore trends — maintain core identity, consistency builds trust
4. Great brands don't chase customers — focus on core customers who share your values
5. Great brands sweat the small stuff — what you DO outweighs what you SAY
6. Great brands commit and stay committed — long-term consistency trumps quick wins
7. Great brands never have to "give back" — purpose is integrated into core operations

### FUSION Framework

When you fuse brand and culture, you create organizational power not possible by cultivating one alone. Foundations: finding overarching purpose and core values, re-thinking core operations and organizational design, intentional employee experience design, corporate rituals and behaviors.

### 9 Brand Types

Disruptive, Conscious, Service, Innovative, Value, Performance, Luxury, Style, Experience. Pick ONE primary type. Each links to specific organizational values.

### Brand Operationalization

Using brand strategy to shape three areas: Culture (embedding values into how the organization operates internally), Operations (integrating brand principles into business processes), Experiences (ensuring brand promise delivery at every touchpoint).

### Core Principles

- Your brand is what you DO, not what you SAY
- Great brands start inside
- There should be no difference between your brand strategy and your business strategy
- Brand power comes from keeping — not only making — promises
- Core values must be USED — otherwise they're neither "core" nor "values"
- Culture and values ARE the brand
- Brand as business management, not brand as communication
