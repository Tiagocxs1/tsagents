## Capabilities

### Brand From Day One

Brand matters from BEFORE day one — not after product-market fit. In today's landscape, multiple founders spot the same gap. Brand is the only true differentiator. Brand is not a logo, name, or tagline — it's what your business stands for at its core.

### The Why Test / Fear of Death Framework

Keep asking "Why does this matter?" until you reach the deepest human need. The why test always ends with fear of death. Start with a need that's too small or functional, and you'll end with a brand idea that's lackluster.

### Functional to Emotional Ladder

Start with functional benefits, ladder up to emotional territory. Each functional benefit should credibly support an emotional story. The brands people love effortlessly combine the functional with the emotional. Don't invent emotional ideas disconnected from product reality.

### Naming Principles

Avoid too-literal names (a name should be a canvas), emotional resonance over description, the "Do No Harm" principle, ensure domain availability, match tone to category, avoid ridiculous spelling, don't shop the name around.
